# Destination Expert — Skill

Apply this knowledge automatically. Never wait to be asked — surface warnings proactively.

## Always flag if relevant to the trip dates
- Peak tourist season: flag crowds and higher prices
- Local public holidays or festivals
- Seasonal weather suitability
- Safety context: districts to avoid, general tourist safety level

## Country-specific rules

### Japan
- Golden Week (Apr 29–May 5): extremely busy, all transport and restaurants need advance booking, prices surge
- Cherry blossom (late March–mid April): beautiful but very crowded — book months ahead
- Typhoon season (August–October): outdoor plans may be disrupted
- Always advise: carry cash in yen (ATMs at 7-Eleven most reliable), get IC Suica card for transit

### Greece and Greek Islands
- August: 35–40°C, peak crowds, highest prices — May/June/Sep/Oct far better
- Siesta: non-tourist shops closed 14:00–17:00
- Ferry strikes: occasional — recommend checking schedules 24h before travel

### Spain / Barcelona / Madrid
- Siesta: shops outside tourist areas often closed 14:00–17:00
- Dinner culture: restaurants rarely open before 20:30, peak dining 22:00
- August: locals leave cities, some neighbourhood restaurants close

### Morocco
- Ramadan: restaurants closed during daylight hours, business schedules adjusted
- Medinas: recommend downloading Google Maps or Maps.me offline before entering
- Haggling: expected in souks, not in fixed-price shops

### Thailand
- Monsoon (May–October): heavy afternoon rain — schedule outdoor activities in mornings
- Songkran (mid-April): vibrant but disruptive to normal travel logistics

### Crete specifically
- CHQ (Chania) and HER (Heraklion) are different airports — confirm which the flight uses
- Hiring a car strongly recommended — public transport between villages is limited
- August sea temperatures: 26–28°C, warmest of the year
